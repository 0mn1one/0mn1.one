Why Detox Your Home?

The average home contains over 500 chemicals. Many of them are in products you use every single day — your cleaning spray, your shampoo, your laundry detergent, even your cookware. These chemicals are linked to hormone disruption, respiratory issues, skin irritation, and long-term health problems. The good news? Swapping them out is simple, affordable, and often cheaper than what you're buying now.

How to Use This Checklist

Go room by room. Each section covers a specific area of your home with the most common toxic products found there.

Check the priority. High items have the biggest health impact — start there. Medium items are worth swapping when they run out. Low items are easy upgrades when you're ready.

Use the swap guide. Every toxic product has a clean alternative listed. Most are things you can make at home for pennies.

Go at your pace. You don't need to throw everything out today. Replace products as they run out. In 3-6 months, your home will be almost entirely toxin-free.

The 5 Worst Offenders

If you only swap five things, make it these:

  • Air fresheners / plug-ins — Contain phthalates and synthetic fragrances. Use essential oils or open windows instead.
  • Non-stick cookware (Teflon) — Releases toxic fumes when heated. Switch to cast iron, stainless steel, or ceramic.
  • Conventional cleaning sprays — Full of ammonia, bleach, and VOCs. Vinegar + water does 90% of the job.
  • Dryer sheets — Coated in synthetic fragrance and chemicals. Use wool dryer balls instead.
  • Plastic food containers — Leach BPA/BPS into food when heated. Switch to glass or stainless steel.

Kitchen

0mn1.one

Where your food is stored, prepared, and cooked. This is the highest-impact room to detox.

Cookware & Food Storage
  • High  Replace non-stick / Teflon pans with cast iron, stainless steel, or ceramic cookware
  • High  Switch plastic food containers to glass or stainless steel (especially for hot food)
  • High  Stop microwaving food in plastic — transfer to a glass or ceramic dish first
  • Med  Replace plastic cutting boards with wood or bamboo
  • Med  Switch plastic wrap to beeswax wraps or silicone lids
  • Low  Replace plastic utensils with wood, bamboo, or stainless steel
Cleaning Products
  • High  Replace conventional dish soap with plant-based, fragrance-free dish soap
  • High  Ditch chemical counter sprays — use vinegar + water solution (1:1 ratio)
  • Med  Replace conventional oven cleaner with baking soda + vinegar paste
  • Med  Switch synthetic sponges to natural cellulose sponges or dish brushes
Water & Beverages
  • High  Install a water filter (countertop, under-sink, or pitcher — removes chlorine, lead, and fluoride)
  • Med  Replace plastic water bottles with glass or stainless steel
  • Low  Switch from plastic coffee makers to French press, pour-over, or stainless steel

DIY All-Purpose Kitchen Cleaner

  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 10 drops lemon essential oil (optional)
  • Spray bottle — that's it. Costs about $0.50 per bottle.

Personal care products absorb directly through your skin. What goes on your body goes in your body.

Personal Care
  • High  Switch conventional deodorant (with aluminum) to natural or mineral deodorant
  • High  Replace shampoo/conditioner with sulfate-free, fragrance-free options
  • High  Switch body wash/soap with synthetic fragrance to castile soap or natural bar soap
  • Med  Replace conventional toothpaste (with triclosan/SLS) with natural toothpaste
  • Med  Switch plastic toothbrush to bamboo toothbrush
  • Med  Replace conventional sunscreen (oxybenzone) with mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide)
  • Low  Switch cotton balls/pads to reusable organic cotton rounds
  • Low  Replace disposable razors with a safety razor (stainless steel, lifetime use)
Bathroom Cleaning
  • High  Replace bleach-based toilet cleaner with baking soda + vinegar or plant-based cleaner
  • High  Ditch chemical shower/tub sprays — baking soda paste + scrub brush works better
  • Med  Replace chemical drain cleaner with baking soda + vinegar + boiling water
  • Low  Switch conventional air freshener to essential oil diffuser or open the window

Ingredients to Avoid on Labels

Parabens (hormone disruptors) — Phthalates (often hidden as "fragrance") — Sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS) (skin irritant) — Triclosan (endocrine disruptor) — Oxybenzone (hormone disruptor, harms coral reefs) — Formaldehyde (carcinogen, found in some hair products)

DIY Bathroom Scrub

  • Enough castile soap to form a paste
  • 10 drops tea tree essential oil (natural disinfectant)
  • Scrub surfaces, rinse. Non-toxic and antibacterial.

Living Room & Bedroom

0mn1.one

Where you breathe, sleep, and recover. Indoor air quality and sleep environment matter more than most people realize.

Living Room
Air Quality
  • High  Remove plug-in air fresheners, scented candles with synthetic fragrance, and aerosol sprays
  • High  Add air-purifying houseplants (snake plant, pothos, spider plant, peace lily)
  • Med  Switch scented candles to beeswax or soy candles with essential oils (or no candles)
  • Med  Open windows daily for 10-15 minutes — even in winter, fresh air circulation matters
  • Low  Consider a HEPA air purifier for the main living area
Surfaces & Furniture
  • Med  Dust with a damp microfiber cloth (not chemical dusting sprays)
  • Med  Vacuum with a HEPA-filter vacuum to trap allergens and particulates
  • Low  When replacing furniture, choose solid wood over pressed board (which off-gasses formaldehyde)
Bedroom
Sleep Environment
  • High  Switch conventional bedsheets to organic cotton, linen, or bamboo (no wrinkle-free chemical treatment)
  • High  Replace synthetic pillow with organic cotton, kapok, buckwheat, or natural latex
  • Med  Remove electronics from the nightstand (reduce EMF exposure near your head)
  • Med  When replacing your mattress, choose organic/natural latex, organic cotton, or wool
  • Low  Use blackout curtains made of natural fabric instead of vinyl-backed curtains

Why Air-Purifying Plants Work

NASA research found that common houseplants can remove up to 87% of air toxins in 24 hours. Snake plants release oxygen at night, making them ideal for bedrooms. Pothos and spider plants are nearly unkillable and filter formaldehyde, benzene, and xylene from indoor air.

Laundry Room

0mn1.one

Laundry products leave chemical residue on every piece of clothing that touches your skin all day long.

Laundry Products
  • High  Replace conventional laundry detergent with plant-based, fragrance-free detergent
  • High  Eliminate dryer sheets — use wool dryer balls (reusable for 1,000+ loads)
  • High  Ditch conventional fabric softener (coats fabric in chemical film) — use white vinegar in the rinse cycle
  • Med  Replace chlorine bleach with oxygen bleach (sodium percarbonate) for whitening
  • Med  Switch stain remover spray to a paste of baking soda + hydrogen peroxide
  • Low  Line-dry clothes when possible — saves energy and avoids dryer chemical exposure

The "Fragrance" Loophole

Companies are not legally required to disclose what's inside the word "fragrance" on a label. A single fragrance can contain up to 3,000 different chemical ingredients — many of them known hormone disruptors, allergens, and neurotoxins. If a product says "fragrance" or "parfum" and doesn't specify what that means, avoid it.

Garage & Storage

Where many of the most toxic products in your home are stored.

  • High  Replace conventional pesticides/herbicides with natural alternatives (diatomaceous earth, neem oil, vinegar)
  • High  Dispose of old paint, solvents, and chemicals at a hazardous waste facility (don't pour down drains)
  • Med  Replace chemical ant/roach killer with borax bait traps or diatomaceous earth
  • Med  Switch chemical weed killer (glyphosate) to boiling water, vinegar spray, or manual pulling
  • Low  Store all chemicals in a well-ventilated area away from living spaces

Glyphosate (Roundup)

The most widely used herbicide in the world is classified as a "probable carcinogen" by the WHO. It's found in most conventional lawns and nearly all non-organic food. Switching to vinegar-based weed control and buying organic produce are two of the highest-impact changes you can make.

Kids, Nursery & Pets

0mn1.one

Children and pets are more vulnerable to chemicals — smaller bodies, faster metabolism, closer to the ground.

Children's Rooms & Nursery
  • High  Use fragrance-free, plant-based baby/kid soap, shampoo, and lotion
  • High  Choose organic cotton crib sheets, pajamas, and blankets (conventional cotton is heavily sprayed)
  • High  Avoid foam mattresses with fire retardant chemicals — choose organic/natural options
  • Med  Use non-toxic, VOC-free paint in children's rooms
  • Med  Choose wood or silicone toys over plastic (especially for teething/mouthing age)
  • Med  Replace baby wipes with water wipes or cloth wipes
  • Low  Use natural diaper cream (zinc oxide-based) instead of petroleum-based
Pet Areas
  • High  Replace chemical flea/tick treatments with natural alternatives (discuss with holistic vet)
  • Med  Switch to stainless steel or ceramic food/water bowls (not plastic)
  • Med  Wash pet bedding with fragrance-free, plant-based detergent
  • Med  Clean pet areas with vinegar solution instead of chemical cleaners (pets lick floors)
  • Low  Choose natural fiber pet beds over synthetic

The Floor Factor

Children under 3 and pets spend most of their time on or near the floor, where heavy chemicals settle. Dust from flame retardants, pesticide residue tracked in on shoes, and off-gassing from carpets concentrate at floor level. Three simple fixes: take shoes off at the door, vacuum with a HEPA filter, and wet-mop with plain water weekly.

The Real Cost

Most clean swaps are either the same price or cheaper than the toxic versions. A $25 cast iron pan lasts a lifetime. A $0.50 vinegar spray replaces a $5 chemical spray every month. The biggest myth about clean living is that it's expensive — it's actually one of the most frugal things you can do.

DIY Recipes & Label Guide

0mn1.one

Save money and control exactly what goes into your products.

All-Purpose Cleaner

  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 10 drops essential oil (lemon, tea tree, or lavender)

Works on counters, glass, appliances. ~$0.50/bottle.

Soft Scrub (Bathroom/Kitchen)

  • 1/2 cup baking soda
  • Liquid castile soap (enough to form paste)
  • 10 drops tea tree oil

Scrubs tubs, sinks, tiles. Natural disinfectant.

Glass Cleaner

  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup rubbing alcohol
  • 1 tbsp white vinegar

Streak-free windows and mirrors.

Laundry Freshener

  • 1/2 cup white vinegar in rinse cycle
  • Wool dryer balls + 3 drops lavender oil

Softens fabric, eliminates static, no chemicals.

Drain Cleaner

  • 1/2 cup baking soda down drain
  • 1/2 cup vinegar — let fizz 15 min
  • Flush with boiling water

Monthly maintenance prevents clogs naturally.

Room Spray / Deodorizer

  • 1 cup distilled water
  • 2 tbsp rubbing alcohol or witch hazel
  • 15-20 drops essential oil of choice

Replaces Febreze, plug-ins, and chemical sprays.

How to Read a Product Label

Red flags on any label: "fragrance" or "parfum" (hidden chemicals), "antibacterial" (usually triclosan), "wrinkle-free" or "stain-resistant" (PFAS chemicals), "flame retardant" (toxic brominated compounds).

Trustworthy certifications: USDA Organic, EWG Verified, MADE SAFE, OEKO-TEX Standard 100, Green Seal, Leaping Bunny (cruelty-free).

Rule of thumb: If you can't pronounce it and it's not a food ingredient, research it before putting it on your skin or in your home.
