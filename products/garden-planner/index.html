Your Garden, Your Way

This planner is designed to help you grow food — whether you have a backyard, a balcony, or just a sunny windowsill. No experience needed. Nature does most of the work. Your job is to show up, pay attention, and enjoy the process.

How to Use This Planner

Planting Calendar — Know exactly when to start seeds indoors, transplant, and direct sow for your zone.

Seed Tracker — Keep track of every variety you're growing: where you got it, when you planted it, how it did.

Garden Layout Grid — Map out your beds, containers, or plot. Plan before you plant.

Companion Planting Chart — Know which plants help each other and which don't get along.

Monthly Log — Record what you did, what worked, and what to change next season.

Harvest Tracker — Track your yields. You'll be surprised how much food you can grow.

One Seed at a Time

You don't need to grow everything. Start with 3-5 things you actually eat. Get those right. Expand next season. The goal isn't perfection — it's connection. Connection to your food, to the soil, to the rhythm of the seasons. That's where the magic is.

The Three Sisters

The oldest companion planting technique: Corn, Beans, and Squash grown together. Corn provides a trellis for beans. Beans fix nitrogen in the soil for all three. Squash shades the ground, retaining moisture and suppressing weeds. Nature's perfect system.

Quick Start Checklist

0mn1.one

First time growing? Follow this list. Keep it simple.

  • Find your growing zone — Search "USDA plant hardiness zone" + your zip code
  • Know your frost dates — Search "last frost date" + your city
  • Pick 3-5 easy crops — Lettuce, radishes, herbs, tomatoes, beans are great starters
  • Choose your space — Raised bed, containers, in-ground, or windowsill
  • Get good soil — Compost + topsoil mix. Soil is everything.
  • Get seeds — Local nursery, seed swap, or online seed companies
  • Plant according to your calendar — Use page 3 of this planner
  • Water consistently — Most plants want 1 inch per week. Morning is best.
  • Mulch everything — Straw, leaves, wood chips. Keeps moisture in, weeds out.
  • Watch and learn — Your garden will teach you. Pay attention.
  • Harvest and enjoy — Pick regularly to encourage more growth
  • Save seeds — One plant can give you seeds for years. That's abundance.
